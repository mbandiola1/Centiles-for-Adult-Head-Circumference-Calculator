Methodology Report: Adult Head Circumference Centile Tool (Model 1)

This tool is based on the linear regression model published in the study: Bushby et al. (1992), assuming a **constant Standard Deviation (SD)** as justified by the authors' final analysis.

I. Core Statistical Model

The calculation relies on three confirmed parameters extracted from the paper:

  1. **Expected Mean Head Circumference ($\mu$):** A gender-specific linear regression equation based on the subject's height.
  2. **Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):** A constant measure of scatter around the mean line ($\text{SD} = 1.41 \text{ cm}$).
  3. **Z-Score:** The number of standard deviations the subject's measurement falls above or below the mean.

II. The Calculation Steps (Constant SD)

Step 1: Calculate the Expected Mean HC ($\mu$)

Males: $\mu = 42.4 + (0.08673 \times \text{Height in cm})$

Females: $\mu = 41.02 + (0.08673 \times \text{Height in cm})$

Step 2: Calculate the Z-Score

The Z-Score (Z) is calculated using the measured HC, the mean $\mu$, and the constant SD:

Z = (Measured HC - $\mu$) / 1.41

Step 3: Calculate the Centile Rank

The centile rank (percentile) is derived by converting the Z-score using the highly accurate **Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)** via the `jStat.normal.cdf` function.

III. Interpretation Thresholds (Clinical Standard)

The thresholds below reflect the standard clinical $\pm 2.0 \text{ SD}$ criteria for abnormality:

  • **Z > +2.0 SD:** **Macrocephaly** (Requires Clinical Review, Red)
  • **Z < -2.0 SD:** **Microcephaly** (Requires Clinical Review, Red)
  • **Z = -2.0 to +2.0 SD:** **Normocephaly** (Within Expected Range, Green)
